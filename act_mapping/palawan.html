El Nido

Why El Nido is Special

El Nido holds a special place in my heart for its breathtaking limestone cliffs that dramatically rise from crystal-clear turquoise waters. The moment I first laid eyes on the Big Lagoon, I was captivated by its surreal beauty. The hidden beaches, accessible only by boat, offer a sense of discovery and isolation that's increasingly rare in today's world.

What makes El Nido truly magical is the way the sunlight plays on the water, creating an ever-changing palette of blues and greens. Swimming through the Small Lagoon, surrounded by towering karst formations, I felt a profound connection with nature that I've rarely experienced elsewhere.

How to Get There

  • Fly to Puerto Princesa International Airport from Manila (1 hour)
  • Take a van or bus from Puerto Princesa to El Nido (5-6 hours)
  • Alternative: Direct flights from Manila to Lio Airport in El Nido (1.5 hours)
  • From El Nido town, join island hopping tours to explore the lagoons and beaches

Coron

Why Coron is Special

Coron captivated me with its perfect blend of natural wonders and historical significance. The Japanese shipwrecks from World War II create an underwater museum that offers a hauntingly beautiful diving experience. Swimming through these time capsules, I felt a profound connection to history.

Kayangan Lake, often called the cleanest lake in Asia, left me speechless with its impossibly clear waters. The climb to the viewpoint overlooking the bay is etched in my memory as one of the most rewarding moments of my travels. The limestone formations create a landscape that seems almost otherworldly.

How to Get There

  • Fly directly from Manila to Francisco B. Reyes Airport in Busuanga (1 hour)
  • Take a van from the airport to Coron Town (30-45 minutes)
  • Alternative: Ferry from El Nido to Coron (3-4 hours)
  • Book island hopping tours to visit Kayangan Lake, Twin Lagoon, and shipwrecks

Puerto Princesa

Why Puerto Princesa is Special

Puerto Princesa holds a special place in my heart as the gateway to Palawan's wonders. The UNESCO World Heritage Underground River left me in awe as I ventured through the cave system by boat, witnessing nature's artistry in the stalactites and stalagmites that have formed over millennia.

Beyond the famous river, I found Puerto Princesa's charm in its commitment to environmental conservation. The city's clean streets and eco-friendly initiatives reflect a deep respect for nature that resonated with my own values. The firefly watching tour along the Iwahig River created a magical evening I'll never forget.

How to Get There

  • Fly directly to Puerto Princesa International Airport from Manila (1.5 hours)
  • Take a tricycle or van to your accommodation in the city
  • Book a tour to the Underground River (1.5-hour drive from the city)
  • Consider island hopping in Honda Bay or exploring Sabang Beach
