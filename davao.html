Malagos Garden Resort

Why Malagos is Special?

Malagos Garden Resort is a nature-themed park famous for its interactive chocolate museum and farm-to-table experiences. It’s a fun and educational destination for all ages.

The resort also features a bird-feeding dome, orchid gardens, and butterfly sanctuary. It’s a refreshing spot to experience agriculture, nature, and Davao’s local charm.

How to Get There?

  • Fly to Davao International Airport (1.5 hours from Manila)
  • Take a taxi or van to Calinan District (1 hour)
  • Located near the Philippine Eagle Center, ideal for a day trip combo

Samal Island

Why Samal Island is Special?

Samal Island is a tropical paradise just minutes away from Davao City, known for its powdery white sand beaches and turquoise waters. It’s the perfect escape for beach lovers and weekend getaways.

Beyond the beaches, you can explore hidden waterfalls, bat caves, and stunning marine life. Whether for luxury or adventure, Samal offers a slice of island bliss.

How to Get There

  • Fly to Davao International Airport from Manila (1.5 hours)
  • Take a taxi or van to Sasa Wharf or Sta. Ana Wharf (20–30 mins)
  • Ride a boat or ferry to Samal Island (10–30 mins travel time)

Philippine Eagle Center

Why Philippine Eagle Center is Special?

The Philippine Eagle Center is a conservation sanctuary that protects the critically endangered Philippine Eagle, the country's national bird. It offers a rare opportunity to see these majestic raptors up close.

The center also houses other species of birds, reptiles, and mammals, promoting awareness and education about Philippine wildlife. It’s a meaningful destination for those who care about biodiversity and environmental preservation.

How to Get There?

  • Fly to Davao International Airport (1.5 hours from Manila)
  • From downtown, take a taxi or van to Calinan District (1 hour)
  • Located inside the Malagos Watershed in Davao City
